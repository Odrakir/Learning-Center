# Mission: Becoming a good engineering manager

## Why
I recently became an engineering manager (first 12 months) and I want to
succeed in the role rather than just survive it. The concrete outcome: my
team ships well, my reports grow and stay, and within this first year I'm
seen as a manager who has clearly got the hang of the job — not someone
still acting like an IC with a title.

## Success looks like
- I run 1:1s my reports actually find valuable (not status meetings).
- My team has a clear, predictable way of planning and shipping work.
- I can give direct feedback — including hard feedback — without dreading it.
- I can explain my team's strategy and priorities up, down, and sideways.
- I can name what a healthy team feels like and spot when mine is drifting.

## Constraints
- ~1 focused hour per week. Lessons must fit that budget.
- Learning by doing: I want to apply each lesson to my real team that week.
- Broad foundations wanted across people, delivery, team health, strategy —
  but taught one tightly-scoped, useful piece at a time.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Managing managers / running a multi-team org (revisit once foundations land).
- Deep hiring/recruiting pipeline mechanics (touch only as it intersects 1:1s).
