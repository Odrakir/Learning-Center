The 1:1 Cheat Sheet

A quick-reference companion to your lessons. Keep it open before each 1:1. Compressed from the resources in RESOURCES.md.

What a 1:1 is for

It is their meeting, not your status update. Status can go async; the 1:1 is for the things that don't fit a ticket: growth, blockers, relationship, and the quiet signals you'd otherwise miss.

The three hats (Lara Hogan)

Most 1:1 misfires come from wearing the wrong hat. Match the hat to what the person needs.

HatYou provideReach for it when…
MentorAdvice from your own experienceThey lack context you have
CoachQuestions that unlock their thinkingThey have the answer in them
SponsorVisibility, opportunity, air coverThey're ready for more reach

Reading the conversation (Rands)

  • The Update — surface status. Useful, but don't let it eat the hour.
  • The Vent — emotion on the surface, a real problem underneath. Listen for the problem.
  • The Disaster — the rare, important one. Drop your agenda and dig in.

Go-to questions

"What's on your mind this week?" — let them set the agenda first.
"What's slowing you down right now?" — surfaces blockers and frustration.
"What would make next week feel like a win?" — orients toward outcomes.
"Where do you want to grow, and are we making room for it?" — growth signal.
"Is there anything I should be doing differently?" — invites upward feedback.

Hygiene

  • Weekly to start; protect the slot, don't be the one who cancels.
  • Keep a shared running doc; both of you add items between meetings.
  • Take notes, especially on growth goals — refer back to them later.
  • Default to listening. If you're talking more than half the time, adjust.
Sources: Lara Hogan — One-on-Ones · Rands — The Update, the Vent, and the Disaster · Fournier — The Manager's Path.
