The 1:1 That Isn't a Status Update

Your most frequent management tool, used well from week one.

As an IC, a meeting was time away from the work. As a manager, the meeting often is the work — and the 1:1 is the one you'll have most. Get it right and you build trust, catch problems early, and grow people. Get it wrong and it quietly decays into a status read-out that neither of you needs.

The core idea

A 1:1 belongs to your report, not to you. Status — what shipped, what's blocked at a ticket level — can travel async or through standup. The 1:1 is for what doesn't fit a ticket: how they're really doing, where they're stuck, where they want to grow, and the early, faint signals you'd otherwise miss until they're loud and expensive.

"The most important thing you can do is establish a regular 1-1 with each of your reports… it builds the relationship that makes everything else possible." — paraphrasing Camille Fournier, The Manager's Path.

Lara Hogan frames the manager's role in a 1:1 as three hats — mentor (give advice), coach (ask questions), and sponsor (create opportunity). Most stumbles come from wearing the wrong one. See the 1:1 Cheat Sheet for when to reach for each.

Your win this week

▸ Prep and run one real 1:1 differently

Before your next actual 1:1:

  1. Open a shared doc with the person (if you don't have one).
  2. Open with "What's on your mind this week?" and stay quiet.
  3. Aim to talk less than half the time. Notice when you're filling silence.
  4. Capture one thing they want to grow toward — you'll revisit it.
