# Engineering Management Resources

## Knowledge

- [Book: _The Manager's Path_ — Camille Fournier](https://www.amazon.com/Managers-Path-Leaders-Navigating-Growth/dp/1491973897)
  The single best starting point for a new EM. Walks the whole ladder (mentor → tech lead → EM → director → exec). Use for: 1:1s, transitioning from IC, managing your first team, what each stage actually requires.
- [Book: _High Output Management_ — Andy Grove](https://www.amazon.com/High-Output-Management-Andrew-Grove/dp/0679762884)
  The origin of the modern 1:1 and "managerial leverage." Dense, foundational. Use for: why 1:1s exist, output thinking, leverage, delegation, task-relevant maturity.
- [Book: _An Elegant Puzzle_ — Will Larson](https://www.amazon.com/Elegant-Puzzle-Systems-Engineering-Management/dp/1732265186)
  Systems-level EM thinking from Uber/Stripe/Carta. Use for: team sizing, org design, tech debt, when problems are systemic not individual. (More relevant once team leadership is solid.)
- [Book: _Become an Effective Software Engineering Manager_ — James Stanier](https://pragprog.com/titles/jsengman/become-an-effective-software-engineering-manager/)
  Very practical, mechanics-first. Use for: 1:1s, feedback, performance reviews, hiring, letting people go.
- [Book: _Radical Candor_ — Kim Scott](https://www.radicalcandor.com/the-book/)
  The canonical feedback model: Care Personally + Challenge Directly. Use for: giving praise and criticism that lands.
- [Site: The Engineering Manager — James Stanier](https://www.theengineeringmanager.com/)
  Free companion essays to the book. Use for: quick, trustworthy refreshers (start with the [Management 101](https://www.theengineeringmanager.com/management-101/) series, e.g. [Delegation](https://www.theengineeringmanager.com/management-101/delegation/)).
- [Site: LeadDev](https://leaddev.com/)
  High-quality articles and talks from practising engineering leaders. Use for: specific scenarios (cross-discipline leadership, feedback, growth).
- [Repo: charlax/engineering-management](https://github.com/charlax/engineering-management)
  Large curated link collection. Use for: finding a deep-dive source on a narrow topic when our shortlist doesn't cover it.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/engineeringmanagement](https://www.reddit.com/r/engineeringmanagement/)
  Active subreddit of practising EMs. Use for: sanity-checking decisions, "how would you handle X" threads.
- [Rands Leadership Slack](https://randsinrepose.com/welcome-to-rands-leadership-slack/)
  Large, well-moderated Slack of engineering leaders (#1-1s, #feedback, #managing-up channels, etc.). Use for: real-time advice from people in the same seat. *(Free; recommended once Ricardo wants live peer input.)*
- [LeadDev community & conferences](https://leaddev.com/community)
  Use for: talks and meetups, occasional in-person learning.
