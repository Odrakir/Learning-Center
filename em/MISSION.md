# Mission: Become a Great Engineering Manager

## Why
Ricardo recently moved from iOS IC to managing a small (2–4 person) team that
spans **iOS, Backend, and QA** — two disciplines he has never practised himself.
He's not fighting a fire; he wants to deliberately build the judgment and habits
of a great EM over the long term, so his team does its best work and grows, and
so he leads with confidence rather than instinct alone.

## Success looks like
- Runs consistent, high-trust 1:1s where reports do most of the talking and leave with support, not status-reporting.
- Can lead, evaluate, and grow Backend and QA engineers **without** being the best technical person in those domains.
- Gives clear, timely feedback (praise and criticism) that lands and changes behaviour.
- Delivers outcomes reliably: knows how to plan, prioritise, and make the team's work legible to others.
- Builds a team that has trust and psychological safety — one people want to stay on.
- Develops a point of view on direction and can influence up and across, not just down.

## Constraints
- New to the role (0–12 months); small team; still close to the iOS work.
- Manages across disciplines he hasn't done — needs leadership techniques that don't depend on out-coding people.
- Long-term growth mindset: depth over quick hacks. Open to all learning styles (frameworks, scenarios, primary sources, drills).
- Works at Goodnotes.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Managing managers / multi-team org design (revisit once team leadership is solid).
- Deep technical upskilling in Backend/QA crafts — the goal is to *lead* them, not to become an expert IC in each.
- Hiring-at-scale and executive strategy.
