# Engineering Management Resources

Curated, high-trust sources for a new engineering manager. Knowledge for
lessons is drawn from here — not from memory. Wisdom comes from the
communities at the bottom.

## Knowledge

- [Book: _The Manager's Path_ — Camille Fournier](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-managers-path/9781491973882/)
  The canonical reference manual for the IC→manager→leader journey. Use for:
  the new-manager chapters, 1:1 expectations, 30/60/90 onboarding plans.
- [Book: _An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management_ — Will Larson](https://press.stripe.com/an-elegant-puzzle)
  Systems-thinking view of EM problems (team sizing, org design, process).
  Use for: delivery, team structure, and scaling questions later in the mission.
- [Blog: Irrational Exuberance — Will Larson (lethain.com)](https://lethain.com/)
  Larson's working notes; many _Elegant Puzzle_ ideas first appeared here, free.
  Use for: deep-dives on specific EM problems with concrete write-ups.
- [Resource hub: One-on-Ones — Lara Hogan](https://larahogan.me/resources/one-on-ones/)
  **Primary source for 1:1s.** Worksheets for mentor/coach/sponsor balance and
  first-1:1 questions. Use for: everything 1:1-related.
- [Article: "Questions for our first 1:1" — Lara Hogan](https://larahogan.me/blog/first-one-on-one-questions/)
  A ready-to-use question set for an initial 1:1. Use for: starting with a new report.
- [Article: "The Update, the Vent, and the Disaster" — Rands in Repose](https://randsinrepose.com/archives/the-update-the-vent-and-the-disaster/)
  Classic lens for what's *really* happening in a 1:1. Use for: reading the room,
  surfacing the real problem under a "vent".
- [Book: _Managing Humans_ — Michael Lopp (Rands)](https://randsinrepose.com/archives/managing-humans/)
  Funny, battle-tested tales of software management. Use for: people situations,
  morale, the human texture of the job.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Rands Leadership Slack](https://randsinrepose.com/welcome-to-rands-leadership-slack/)
  Large, well-moderated Slack for eng leaders. Use for: "has anyone dealt with…"
  questions and sanity-checks on real situations.
- [LeadDev](https://leaddev.com/)
  Global learning community (newsletter, talks, conferences) for eng leadership.
  Use for: talks and articles on specific leadership challenges.
- [r/engineeringmanagers](https://www.reddit.com/r/engineeringmanagers/)
  Public subreddit. Use for: lower-stakes peer perspective; weigh signal carefully.
